Skilled-Jobs English Tests — Reading & Writing (A1, A2, B1)

Instructions for examiners: each candidate completes the reading sections (choose 1 or all depending on time) and the writing tasks. Multiple-choice questions are auto-graded; writing tasks show word count and checklist-based rubric for manual scoring.

A1 — Beginner: Reading Comprehension (2 short tests)

Test 1 — Passage (A1)

Anna works in a small bakery. Every morning she opens the shop at 6:00 a.m. She bakes bread and sells coffee. Her friend Mark helps on weekends. The bakery is near a bus stop, so many people come to buy breakfast.

1. What time does Anna open the shop?
2. Who helps Anna on weekends?
3. Short answer: Where is the bakery located?
Test 2 — Passage (A1)

Tom is a plumber. He fixes taps and pipes. Today he goes to a house. The family says thank you because the water works again. Tom has lunch at 12:30 and returns to the office at 2:00.

1. What is Tom's job?
2. Short answer: When does Tom have lunch?

A2 — Elementary: Reading Comprehension (2 short tests)

Test 1 — Passage (A2)

Maria moved to Sydney last year to work as an electrician. She likes the city because she can walk to the beach on weekends. On weekdays she travels by train. Maria studies for a trade certificate in the evenings and hopes to become a team leader in two years.

1. Why does Maria like the city?
2. Short answer: What does Maria hope to become?
Test 2 — Passage (A2)

Job ads often ask for two things: experience and a current visa. If the applicant has experience but no visa, it is difficult to hire them. Many employers prefer candidates who can start quickly and speak clear English.

1. What do job ads often ask for?
2. Short answer: Why do employers prefer some candidates?

B1 — Intermediate: Reading Comprehension (2 tests)

Test 1 — Passage (B1)

Many skilled workers move between cities to follow job opportunities. Relocating can be positive — it often brings higher pay and new skills — but it also has costs: finding housing, changing schools for children, and the time needed to settle in. Employers who offer relocation support are more likely to attract experienced candidates. Some companies give a relocation allowance, others help with temporary accommodation.

1. Which of the following is NOT listed as a cost of relocating?
2. Short answer: How do some companies support relocating employees?
Test 2 — Passage (B1)

Workplace safety is important in trades such as construction and manufacturing. Australian regulations require employers to provide protective equipment and training. Workers should report hazards immediately so managers can act. Regular safety meetings help teams stay aware of risks and improve procedures.

1. What must employers provide according to the passage?
2. Short answer: Why are regular safety meetings useful?

Writing Tests — Essay & Email (for skilled-job applicants)

Candidates should do both tasks. For Australia skilled-job assessment, encourage clear organization, relevant ideas, and appropriate tone. Use the rubric below for marking.

Writing Rubric (suggested):
  • Task Response / Content (0–6): relevance, development of ideas, completeness.
  • Coherence & Cohesion (0–4): clear paragraphing, logical flow, linking words.
  • Range & Accuracy of Grammar (0–6): correct sentence structures, verb tenses.
  • Vocabulary & Spelling (0–4): appropriate word choice, correct spelling.

Total suggested score: 0–20 (convert to your preferred scale).

Essay (recommended 200–300 words)

Prompt: "Describe a time when you improved a process at work. Explain what the problem was, what steps you took to change the process, and what the result was. If you could do it again, what would you change?"

Purpose: test organisation, range of vocabulary related to work, use of past tenses and conditional language.

Formal Email (recommended 80–150 words)

Prompt: "You have been offered a skilled-job interview in Australia but the time clashes with your current job. Write a polite email to the recruitment officer asking to reschedule the interview and provide two alternative dates and times."

Purpose: test register (formal tone), clarity, ability to propose alternatives.

Suggested Examiner Checklist (use for manual marking)
  1. Does the writing fully answer the prompt?
  2. Is the structure clear (intro, body, conclusion / opening, ask, alternatives, closing)?
  3. Are there frequent grammar errors that impede understanding?
  4. Is vocabulary appropriate for a workplace / professional email?
  5. Word count within recommended range?

Tip: For official assessment use, combine these tests with an oral interview and human marking for writing. Automated checks here are supportive only.
